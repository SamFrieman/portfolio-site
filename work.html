World Insurance Associates LLC June 2025 – August 2025
Hunting Hidden Risk in 40+ M&A Targets
Situation
WIA was accelerating an aggressive M&A strategy, evaluating 40+ insurance companies for acquisition. Each target carried unknown cybersecurity debt—SQL injection vectors, exposed services, misconfigured cloud assets—that could become WIA's liability the moment a deal closed. No systematic OSINT process existed to assess this risk at scale before due diligence concluded.
Task
Build and execute a repeatable passive reconnaissance methodology to surface critical vulnerabilities across all targets, then communicate findings directly to the CISO in a format that could inform go/no-go acquisition decisions.
Action
I cross-referenced Shodan, Censys, and VirusTotal to map each target's internet-exposed attack surface without touching their systems. I standardized a scoring rubric across SSL/TLS hygiene, exposed admin panels, leaked credentials on paste sites, and subdomain enumeration results.
I chose passive OSINT over active scanning deliberately — active scans against pre-acquisition targets could have legal implications and tipped off targets. Passive techniques gave us 80% of the risk picture with zero legal exposure and zero detection.
For web applications, I combined automated Nuclei scans with manual testing to confirm vulnerabilities before escalation — I never reported a finding I hadn't validated, because a false positive in M&A due diligence wastes executive time and can derail deals.
Result
Uncovered 40+ critical findings including SQL injection vectors and XSS vulnerabilities across multiple targets. Findings were presented directly to the CISO with risk-ranked remediation timelines. Two targets had findings serious enough to factor into deal valuation conversations.

World Insurance Associates LLC June 2025 – August 2025
Turning 300+ Controls into a 35% Security Improvement
Situation
WIA was deploying Microsoft's enterprise security stack — Entra ID, Defender, Intune — across the organization. They had a NIST 800-53 compliance requirement but no clear mapping between the 300+ controls and their actual Microsoft environment. The gap analysis had never been completed.
Task
Audit the current security posture against NIST 800-53, identify control gaps, and develop a remediation roadmap that prioritized the highest-risk gaps first — balancing security improvement against operational disruption.
Action
I systematically worked through the 800-53 control families, mapping each control to its Microsoft implementation (or lack thereof). For controls with partial implementation, I documented the gap with a severity rating based on likelihood × impact.
The hardest part wasn't technical — it was learning to translate security control language into business terms. "AC-2: Account Management" means nothing to a CFO. I had to reframe every finding in terms of breach risk, regulatory penalty exposure, and operational downtime to get prioritization decisions approved quickly.
I then assisted directly with the Entra ID and Intune configuration, testing conditional access policies in a staging environment before production rollout to avoid locking out users — a mistake I'd seen happen at other organizations.
Result
Overall security posture improved by 35% measured against the NIST baseline. Remediation roadmap gave the CISO a 90-day, 180-day, and 12-month plan with cost estimates — enabling board-level reporting for the first time.

Windward Consulting LLC Oct. 2023 – Sept. 2024
Reclaiming 5 Hours a Week From Manual SOC Work
Situation
The SOC team was processing 100+ data points weekly through manual CSV manipulation and report generation. Analysts with deep threat expertise were spending significant time on mechanical formatting tasks — alert fatigue was real, and it was getting worse. Genuine threats were at risk of being missed because cognitive bandwidth was exhausted on repetitive work.
Task
Identify the highest-ROI automation opportunities, build the tooling, and validate that it actually freed up analyst time without introducing new failure modes in the reporting pipeline.
Action
I spent two weeks shadowing analysts to understand exactly where time was lost before writing a single line of code. I mapped the full workflow: data ingestion, normalization, enrichment, and report generation — identifying which steps were purely mechanical vs. which required judgment.
I chose Python over PowerShell for the core pipeline because the data manipulation requirements favored pandas over CSV handling, even though PowerShell would have been faster for the Windows team to maintain. I documented everything and provided PowerShell wrappers so the team could trigger scripts without touching Python directly.
I also conducted a comparative evaluation of Nessus vs. OpenVAS across 40+ scanning techniques to inform the team's platform decision — building a decision matrix that weighed cost, coverage, integration complexity, and false positive rates.
Result
Scripts reduced weekly reporting time by 3–5 hours per analyst. The vulnerability scanner evaluation gave the team a documented, defensible platform recommendation — removing months of indecision. Analysts reported meaningfully less cognitive fatigue on high-volume weeks.

Karen J. Solofsky OD June 2021 – August 2023
Zero HIPAA Incidents — The Hard Way and the Smart Way
Situation
A medical practice managing 5,000+ patient records with staff who had no security training and no reason to care about it. Phishing was the primary attack vector, and the staff clicked. HIPAA penalties for a breach start at $100 per record — a single incident could cost $500K+. The practice had no budget for enterprise security tooling.
Task
Reduce phishing susceptibility, harden the web presence against exploitation, and maintain HIPAA compliance — all without disrupting patient-facing operations or requiring a security budget that didn't exist.
Action
On the human side: instead of slide-deck training, I created realistic phishing simulations tailored to the practice's actual vendors and patient communication patterns. When staff clicked, they got immediate, contextual feedback rather than a quarterly lecture.
The resistance was real. Staff felt surveilled and embarrassed when they failed simulations. I learned to reframe the program as "catching the attacker, not catching you" — shifting blame from the individual to the phishing technique. Participation and attitude shifted within two months.
On the technical side: I audited the practice website and found XSS vulnerabilities and weak SSL/TLS configuration. I implemented security headers, enforced TLS 1.2+, and patched the XSS vectors — all changes that also improved Google's security scoring and page load performance.
Result
Phishing click rate dropped 60% over six months. Zero HIPAA security incidents across two years of operation. Website security hardening delivered a 40% performance improvement as a byproduct — faster load times, better patient experience.
