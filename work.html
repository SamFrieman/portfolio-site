World Insurance Associates LLC June 2025 to August 2025
Hunting Hidden Risk Across 40+ M&A Targets
WIA was evaluating 40+ insurance companies for acquisition with no systematic security review in the pipeline. Undetected SQL injection, exposed admin panels, and misconfigured cloud assets in a target become WIA's liability the moment a deal closes.
I built a passive OSINT methodology using Shodan, Censys, and VirusTotal to map each target's internet-exposed attack surface without touching their systems. Passive-only was a deliberate choice: active scanning creates legal exposure during pre-authorization recon and tips off acquisition targets. I standardized a scoring rubric across SSL/TLS hygiene, exposed admin panels, leaked credentials on paste sites, and subdomain enumeration results.

For web applications, I combined Nuclei scans with manual testing to confirm findings before escalation. I never reported anything I hadn't validated, because a false positive in M&A due diligence wastes executive time and can derail deals.
Surfaced 40+ critical findings including SQL injection vectors and XSS vulnerabilities across multiple targets. Findings went directly to the CISO with risk-ranked remediation timelines. Two targets had findings serious enough to factor into deal valuation conversations.

World Insurance Associates LLC June 2025 to August 2025
Turning 300+ Controls into a 35% Security Improvement
WIA was deploying Microsoft's enterprise security stack (Entra ID, Defender, Intune) with a NIST 800-53 compliance requirement and no mapping between the 300+ controls and their actual environment. The gap analysis had never been completed.
I worked systematically through the 800-53 control families, mapping each control to its Microsoft implementation (or noting the gap). For controls with partial implementation, I documented the gap with a severity rating based on likelihood times impact.

The hardest part wasn't technical: it was translating security control language into business terms. "AC-2: Account Management" means nothing to a CFO. I reframed every finding in terms of breach risk, regulatory penalty exposure, and operational downtime to get prioritization decisions approved quickly. I then assisted directly with Entra ID and Intune configuration, testing conditional access policies in staging before production rollout.
I tested all conditional access policies in a staging environment first. Locking out users in production is a mistake I had seen happen at other organizations, and it destroys trust in the security team immediately.
Security posture improved 35% against the NIST baseline. Remediation roadmap gave the CISO a 90-day, 180-day, and 12-month plan with cost estimates, enabling board-level reporting for the first time.

Windward Consulting LLC October 2023 to September 2024
Reclaiming 5 Hours a Week From Manual SOC Work
The SOC team was processing 100+ data points weekly through manual CSV manipulation and report generation. Analysts with deep threat expertise were spending significant time on mechanical formatting. Alert fatigue was real, and genuine threats risked being missed because cognitive bandwidth was exhausted on repetitive work.
I spent two weeks shadowing analysts to understand exactly where time was lost before writing a single line of code. I mapped the full workflow: data ingestion, normalization, enrichment, and report generation, identifying which steps were purely mechanical versus which required judgment.
I chose Python over PowerShell for the core pipeline because the data manipulation requirements favored pandas over CSV handling, even though PowerShell would have been faster for the Windows team to maintain. I documented everything and provided PowerShell wrappers so the team could trigger scripts without touching Python directly.
I also ran a comparative evaluation of Nessus versus OpenVAS across 40+ scanning techniques to inform the team's platform decision, building a decision matrix that weighed cost, coverage, integration complexity, and false positive rates.
Scripts reduced weekly reporting time by 3 to 5 hours per analyst. The vulnerability scanner evaluation gave the team a documented, defensible platform recommendation and removed months of indecision. Analysts reported meaningfully less cognitive fatigue on high-volume weeks.

Karen J. Solofsky OD June 2021 to August 2023
Zero HIPAA Incidents Across Two Years
A medical practice managing 5,000+ patient records with staff who had no security training. Phishing was the primary attack vector, and staff clicked. HIPAA penalties for a breach start at $100 per record: a single incident could cost $500,000 or more. No budget existed for enterprise security tooling.
On the human side: instead of slide-deck training, I created realistic phishing simulations tailored to the practice's actual vendors and patient communication patterns. When staff clicked, they received immediate, contextual feedback rather than a quarterly lecture.

The resistance was real. Staff felt surveilled and embarrassed when they failed simulations. I reframed the program as catching the attacker, not catching the employee, shifting blame from the individual to the phishing technique. Participation and attitude shifted within two months.

On the technical side: I audited the practice website and found XSS vulnerabilities and weak SSL/TLS configuration. I implemented security headers, enforced TLS 1.2+, and patched the XSS vectors. All changes that also improved Google's security scoring and page load performance.
Phishing click rate dropped 60% over six months. Zero HIPAA security incidents across two years. Website security hardening delivered a 40% performance improvement as a byproduct, with faster load times and better patient experience.
